    Eight dead and thousands injured after Hezbollah radio devices explode

    Lebanese minister condemns “Israeli aggression’’ after Iranian ambassador, Mojtaba Amani, is injured in Beirut in militia’s ‘biggest security breach yet’
    new
    Samer Al-Atrush, Middle East Correspondent
    Tuesday September 17 2024, 4.45pm BST, The Times
    Samer Al-Atrush, Middle East Correspondent
    Tuesday September 17 2024, 4.45pm BST, The Times

    At least eight people were killed in Lebanon and more than 2,750 others injured, including Hezbollah fighters, medics and Iran’s envoy to Beirut, when radio communication devices used by members of the militia exploded in its “biggest security breach” yet.

    The Lebanese information minister, Ziad Makary, condemned “Israeli aggression”. Suspicion immediately fell on an Israeli intelligence operation that may have penetrated the Iranian-backed group, which has been conducting almost daily cross-border attacks on Israel since the war in Gaza broke out last October.
